George Stinnley Jr.

This boy by the name of George Stinnley Jr was the youngest person in American history to be executed. He did nothing wrong. He was wrongfully murdered for a crime he did not commit and the people of North Carolina knew that. He was convicted for the murders of two little girls and was sentences to the electric chair. His family could not see his execution because the people of the small town threatened them if they did not leave town. It took the jury ten minutes to convict this 14, 90 pound boy of a murder. It also turned out that one of the jurors knew who was the killer this entire time and allowed this boy to get killed. George's attorney was no help either. On the day of his execution, he was too short to reach the electric chair so the guards made him sit on a bible. Again, he was only 14 years old.
